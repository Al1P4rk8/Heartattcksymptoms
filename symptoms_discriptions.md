-Chest Pain/Pressure
    **What it might feel like:**
        - A heavy weight or elephant sitting on your chest
        - A tight band squeezing around your ribs
        - Deep ache or pressure (not always sharp pain)

    **What it's usually NOT:**
        - Brief, sharp pains that come and go in seconds

-shortness of breath
    **what is might feel like**
        -"Gasping" or "choking": A severe and sudden difficulty with breathing.
        -Effortful breathing: You may feel like you are working harder to breathe.
        -Chest tightness or pressure: it feels like a heavy, squeezing, or crushing sensation in the chest.
    **what its usually NOT:**
       Normal breathlessness after exercise or climbing stairs .
-Arm/shoulder pain
    **what it might feel like**
     Described as a feeling of pressure, squeezing, heaviness, or a dull ache, rather than a sharp or stabbing pain
     **what it shouldnt feel like**
     Sharp pain that is very focal and can be pinpointed with one finger is often not heart-related
-sweating 
    **what its might feel like**
    Sudden, heavy sweating or a "cold sweat" that occurs without physical exertion, high temperatures, or an obvious reason. temperature down.
    **What it is usually not**
     Sweating that is a normal response to exercise, hot weather, or a fever
-Nausea
    **What it might feel like**
    A feeling of sickness (nausea) or actual vomiting that comes on suddenly and has no clear cause.
    **What it is usually not** Nausea or sickness associated with food poisoning, a stomach bug
-Back pain
    **What it feels like**
    A dull ache, pressure, or tightness in the upper back, often between the shoulder blades. It may feel like a rope tied around the chest and back. The pain typically does not change with movement or position and may come and go.
    **What it is usually not**
    Sharp, localized pain that worsens when you press on the area or change positions, which is more likely a muscle strain or nerve issue.
-Jaw pain
    **What it feels like**
     Aching, tightness, or general discomfort in the jaw, neck, or even teeth. The pain is often diffuse (hard to pinpoint the exact location) and may radiate from the chest or arms.
    **What it is usually not**
    Pain that can be pinpointed to a single tooth, a specific joint 
-Unusual fatigue
    **What it feels like**
     An overwhelming and unexplained exhaustion or tiredness that is new and unusual for you. It might be a severe lack of energy that limits your ability to perform simple, everyday activities, like walking to the mailbox.
    **What it is usually not** 
    Normal tiredness after a long, busy day or strenuous activity that is relieved by rest and sleep. 
-Dizziness
    **What it feels like** 
    A sudden feeling of lightheadedness, unsteadiness, or like you might faint, especially if it occurs with other symptoms like shortness of breath or chest discomfort.
    **What it is usually not**
     Momentary lightheadedness from standing up too quickly
-Indigestion/stomach discomfort
    **What it feels like as a heart attack symptom**
     Discomfort in the upper abdomen that may feel like heartburn, fullness, or a bad upset stomach. Unlike typical indigestion, it may persist for more than a few minutes, come and go, and is not necessarily related to eating a large meal.
    **What it is usually not**
    A burning sensation that travels to the throat (classic acid reflux) and is quickly and consistently relieved by antacids
